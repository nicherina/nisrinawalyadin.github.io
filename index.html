Ultra-fast (≥100kW) stations represent a small but critical fraction — these are IONITY-tier chargers enabling long-distance EV travel.

🗺️ Geographic Cluster Map

● Cluster Hub ● Desert Zone ● Ultra-Fast

K-Means clustering (k=6) identifies regional infrastructure hubs and charging deserts — areas with low ultra-fast penetration.

🔍 Methodology

Data sourced from Open Charge Map API (30,000+ stations). Cleaned and parsed into structured CSV covering coordinates, operator, power output, connection types, and operational status.

K-Means clustering applied to lat/lon coordinates. Optimal k selected via elbow method and silhouette score. Charging deserts defined as clusters with <5% ultra-fast (≥100kW) penetration.

💡 Business Relevance

This analysis directly mirrors how EV network operators like IONITY plan infrastructure expansion:

→ Identify where gaps exist in ultra-fast coverage
→ Quantify underserved regions by population and area
→ Prioritise new station placement based on cluster data
→ Track competitor coverage by operator

EV Adoption Forecast (2025–2029)

📈 Forecast Comparison (2010–2029)

Three models compared on test set MAE. Prophet includes confidence intervals — useful for infrastructure planning under uncertainty.

Bayern and Baden-Württemberg lead in absolute EV stock. Eastern states show lower adoption per capita — indicating potential growth markets.

🔍 Methodology

Data sourced from Kraftfahrtbundesamt (KBA) and EAFO — official German vehicle registration data (2010–2024).

Three models trained on 2010–2021 data, validated on 2022–2024:
→ Polynomial Linear Regression (degree=2)
→ ARIMA (1,1,1) — classical time series
→ Prophet — handles trend breaks, gives confidence intervals

💡 Business Relevance

Demand forecasting is critical for IONITY's expansion strategy:

→ Predict how many EVs will need charging by 2029
→ Identify which regions will see fastest growth
→ Size infrastructure investment accordingly
→ Compare Germany vs EU peers on adoption trajectory
